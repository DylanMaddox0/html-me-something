Introduction

For as long as I can remeber I have always had ear problems. From when I was an infant until I was 15 I had tubes. For those who dont know that is wierd usualy you stop getting tubes put in your ears at the age of 12. Last but not least when I was 11 I had a surgey to remove a cholesteotoma which is a noncancerous skin tumor. For the five days prior to this surgery I got a extremely painful migraine. On the day before the surgery the migrains finally stopped. Turns out the reason I had the migrains was because I had a rare disease called a meningeocephlocele. This itself is not rare but because the cause was the cholesteotoma it was very rare. After the surgery the doctor told my mom that it was so rare that he had only ever read about it in books.

What is a meningeocephlocele?

A meningeocephloceleis a protrusion of the meninges and brain through a congenital defect in the cranium, usually in the frontal or occipital

What are the casues?

The main causes of a meningeocephlocele are unknown but it is a birth defect and is believed to be caused by enviromental factors. Usually but in my case it was caused by the cholesteotoma which is super rare.

What are the symptoms?

Symptoms of a meningeocephlocele are csf leakage, headaches, and nasal obstruction.

What are the treatment options and lasting effects?

The only treatment option for a meningeocephlocele is surgery. Because of the dangerous nature of it you have to get it removed asap. Also 75 percent of people who survive the surgery have some kind of mental deficit. Luckily in my case I had no adverse effects.
